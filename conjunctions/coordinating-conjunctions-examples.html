Coordinating Conjunction Examples

Coordinating conjunctions are a part of speech that connects words, phrases, or clauses that are grammatically equal. This includes connections between words, between phrases, and between clauses. Below are examples of coordinating conjunctions:

'And' examples

  • I like tea and coffee.
  • He's a great singer and dancer.
  • The team played hard and won the game.

'But' examples

  • I tried to lift the box, but it was too heavy.
  • She loves her job, but she doesn't like her boss.
  • He wanted to go to the party, but he wasn't invited.

'Or' examples

  • You can have ice cream or cake for dessert.
  • Would you prefer to travel by train or by plane?
  • Should I wear the blue shirt or the red one?
