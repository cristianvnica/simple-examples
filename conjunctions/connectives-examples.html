Connectives Examples

Connectives, also known as conjunctions or linking words, are used to connect ideas, sentences, and paragraphs smoothly. They're crucial for maintaining coherence and cohesion in both written and spoken language. Here are some examples, grouped by their general purpose:

Adding Information

  • And
  • Also
  • As well as
  • Moreover
  • Furthermore
  • Plus
  • Additionally
  • Besides
  • Not to mention

Contrasting Ideas

  • But
  • However
  • On the other hand
  • Conversely
  • Nevertheless
  • Still
  • Yet
  • Despite that
  • Although

Providing Examples or Illustrating

  • For example
  • For instance
  • Such as
  • In particular
  • Namely
  • That is (i.e.)
  • To illustrate

Showing Cause and Effect or Result

  • So
  • Therefore
  • As a result
  • Consequently
  • Hence
  • Thus
  • That's why

Sequencing Ideas or Time Connectives

  • First, second, third, etc.
  • Next
  • Then
  • After
  • Before
  • Previously
  • Later
  • Meanwhile
  • Finally

Comparing Ideas

  • Similarly
  • Likewise
  • In the same way
  • Just as

Expressing Conditions

  • If
  • Unless
  • Provided that
  • Assuming that
  • In case

Summarizing Ideas

  • In summary
  • To summarize
  • In conclusion
  • To conclude
  • In short
  • Briefly

Connectives are utilized to enhance the clarity and logic in your communication, so it's important to select the ones that most appropriately bridge the ideas you aim to connect.
