Subordinating Conjunction Examples

Subordinating conjunctions are a part of speech in English and many other languages. They are used to connect a subordinate clause to a main clause, adding more information and details to the sentence.

Subordinating Conjunctions

  • Because: She went to the store because she needed milk.
  • Although: Although it was raining, he walked to the school.
  • While: She read a book while she was waiting.
  • Since: I haven’t seen him since we graduated from high school.
  • Until: You cannot go out until you finish your homework.
  • As: She is good at painting as she took many art classes.
  • If: You will pass the test if you study hard.
