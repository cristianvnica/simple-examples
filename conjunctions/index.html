Conjunction Examples

Conjunctions, also known as connectives, are a part of speech in English and many other languages. They are used to connect words, phrases, clauses, or sentences together, making the text easier to read and the ideas easier to understand.

They're a fundamental part of grammar and are typically divided into different categories based on their function. Some common categories include:

Adding Information

  • Coordinating Conjunctions: These connect words, phrases, or clauses that are grammatically equal, and include words such as 'and', 'but', and 'or'.
  • Subordinating Conjunctions: These link a subordinate clause to a main clause. Examples include 'because', 'although', 'while', and 'since'.
  • Correlative Conjunctions: These are pairs of conjunctions that work together, like 'either/or', 'neither/nor', 'both/and', and 'not only/but also'.
  • Conjunctive Adverbs: These are words that join two independent clauses together. Examples include 'however', 'therefore', 'furthermore', and 'consequently'.

Each category serves different purposes in linking thoughts and ideas, helping to create coherence in the text and aid in the fluidity of reading or speaking.
