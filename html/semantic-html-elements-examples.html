Semantic HTML elements examples

Here are examples of most popular semantic HTML elements:

<header>

Represents the header of a webpage or a section, typically containing logos and navigation. 

  <header>
    <h1>My Website</h1>
    <nav>
      <!-- Navigation links go here -->
    </nav>
  </header>

<nav>

Represents a section containing navigation links. 

  <nav>
    <ul>
      <li><a href="/">Home</a></li>
      <li><a href="/about">About</a></li>
      <li><a href="/contact">Contact</a></li>
    </ul>
  </nav>

<main>

Represents the main content of a webpage, excluding headers, footers, and sidebars. 

  <main>
    <h2>Welcome to My Website</h2>
    <p>This is the main content of the page.</p>
  </main>

<article>

Represents a self-contained piece of content, like a blog post or news article. 

  <article>
    <h2>Blog Post Title</h2>
    <p>Published on May 9, 2023</p>
    <p>Here is the content of the blog post...</p>
  </article>

<section>

Represents a thematic grouping of content, typically with a heading. 

  <section>
    <h2>About Us</h2>
    <p>Information about the company goes here.</p>
  </section>

<aside>

Represents content that is tangentially related to the content around it, like a sidebar or pull quote. 

    <aside>
      <h2>Did You Know?</h2>
      <p>Interesting fact related to the main content.</p>
    </aside>

<footer>

Represents the footer of a webpage or section, typically containing copyright information and contact details. 

    <footer>
      <p>Copyright &copy; 2023 My Website. All rights reserved.</p>
    </footer>
